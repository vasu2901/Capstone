About Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that originates in the cells of the breast tissue. It occurs when abnormal cells in the breast grow and divide uncontrollably, forming a mass or lump. Breast cancer is a significant health concern, affecting both men and women, but it is more common in women.

Types of Breast Cancer

  1. Benign Breast Cancer

    • Benign tumors are non-cancerous growths that do not invade nearby tissues or spread to other parts of the body.
    • These tumors are typically slow-growing and do not pose a significant threat to health.

    • While benign tumors are not cancerous, they may still require medical attention if they cause discomfort or other symptoms.

  2. Malignant Breast Cancer

    • Malignant tumors are cancerous and have the potential to invade surrounding tissues and spread to other parts of the body through the bloodstream or lymphatic system.
    • The most common type of breast cancer is invasive ductal carcinoma, which starts in the milk ducts and can invade nearby tissues in the breast.
    • Other types include invasive lobular carcinoma, inflammatory breast cancer, and triple-negative breast cancer, among others.
    • Early detection and treatment are crucial for effectively managing malignant breast cancer.

Prevention

  • Regular breast self-exams and clinical breast exams.
  • Mammograms for early detection of abnormalities.
  • Leading a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and regular exercise.
  • Risk-reducing medications and prophylactic surgeries for high-risk individuals.
